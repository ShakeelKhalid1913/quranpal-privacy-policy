QuranPal Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

Introduction

QuranPal ("we", "our", or "us") is a Quran reading and listening app. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, and what choices you have. By using QuranPal, you agree to this policy.

Information We Collect

Account information (optional)

If you sign in, we use Firebase Authentication to create and manage your account. Depending on how you sign in, this may include:

  • Email address
  • Display name
  • Account provider (email/password, Google, or Apple)
  • A unique user ID assigned by Firebase

Data you create in the app

When you are signed in, the following may be stored on your device and synced to Google Cloud Firestore under your account so you can restore it on another device:

  • Quran reading progress (main goal, side reading)
  • Bookmarks and bookmark folders
  • Liked verses
  • Prayer tracking logs (which prayers you marked as completed, by date)

Data stored only on your device

Some settings and progress stay locally on your phone using SharedPreferences, including:

  • Theme and reading view preferences
  • Selected translations, tafsir, and reciter
  • Audio playback position (listen tab)
  • Night recitation and event reading progress
  • Cached prayer times
  • Prayer calculation method and madhab (school) settings
  • Onboarding completion state

If you are not signed in, prayer logs and reading progress remain on your device only.

Location (optional)

If you allow location access, QuranPal uses your coordinates to fetch prayer times from the Aladhan API. We store latitude, longitude, and city name locally on your device to show times and cache results. We do not sell location data.

Technical and service data

Firebase and our hosting providers may process standard technical data needed to run the app (for example device type, app version, and error logs). We use Firebase Auth and Cloud Firestore to provide sign-in and sync.

How We Use Your Information

  • Provide Quran reading, listening, bookmarks, and prayer features
  • Sync your progress and saved content when you are signed in
  • Calculate and display prayer times for your area
  • Maintain app security and fix bugs

We do not use your data for advertising or sell your personal information.

Third-Party Services

QuranPal relies on these services to function:

  • Firebase (Google) — authentication and cloud database
  • Aladhan API — prayer time calculations
  • EveryAyah / MP3Quran — streaming Quran audio
  • Google Sign-In / Sign in with Apple — optional login

Each provider has its own privacy policy governing how they handle data.

Donations

QuranPal is free. Optional tips are processed by Google Play or the Apple App Store when you choose to support the app. We do not collect or store your payment card details in QuranPal.

Internet Access

The app needs internet access to stream audio, sync signed-in data, sign in, and load prayer times. Bundled Quran text and translations are stored in the app; some tafsir content may require a network connection.

Data Retention and Deletion

  • Local data is removed when you uninstall the app.
  • Cloud data tied to your account remains until you delete it or request account deletion.
  • You can sign out at any time from Profile settings.

To request deletion of your QuranPal account and associated cloud data, follow the steps on our Account Deletion page or email programmer191370070@gmail.com.

Children's Privacy

QuranPal is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect more information from children than is needed to use the app's features. If you believe a child has provided personal data without appropriate consent, contact us and we will take steps to delete it.

Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you. Contact us using the email below to make a request.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the new version on this page and update the "Last updated" date.
